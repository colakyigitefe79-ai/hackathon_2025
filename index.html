EU Passes Groundbreaking Textile Waste Legislation

Policy Update December 15, 2024 By Sarah Johnson

The European Union has unanimously passed comprehensive legislation that will revolutionize how the textile industry handles waste, marking the most significant environmental policy change in the sector's history.

The new regulations, which will take effect in January 2026, require all fashion brands operating in EU markets to implement extended producer responsibility (EPR) schemes. This means companies will be financially responsible for the entire lifecycle of their products, from production to disposal.

Key Provisions

  • Mandatory collection and recycling targets of 65% by 2030
  • Prohibition of destroying unsold textiles
  • Digital product passports for traceability
  • Minimum recycled content requirements

Industry experts predict this legislation will drive innovation in sustainable materials and circular business models, potentially reducing textile waste by up to 40% within the next decade.
